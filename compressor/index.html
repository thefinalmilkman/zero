Why "no upload" matters, and how this shrinks a file

Your photo stays yours. Most free compressors upload your image to a server you don't control — which means a stranger's computer now has a copy of your photo, its metadata, and whatever's in it. This tool never sends it anywhere. There is no server.

How it works: your browser re-draws the image and re-encodes it at the quality you choose. Lower quality and smaller dimensions mean a smaller file. JPEG and WebP throw away detail your eye barely notices; PNG stays lossless but bigger. Slide until the size and the look both feel right.
