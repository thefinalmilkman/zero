P1085 — Fuel Control Mixture Lean (Bank 2, Sensor 1)

CAUTIONIs it safe to drive? Drive gently and get it looked at soon — it can worsen or fail inspection.

What P1085 means

This is a manufacturer-specific code — its meaning depends on the vehicle’s make (see below).

What P1085 means on specific makes

BMW / Mini CAUTION

Same lean condition as P1083 but on Bank 2. P1083 and P1085 nearly always appear together on BMW inline-6s, which points to a shared cause (CCV or vacuum leak) rather than one bank.

  1. Crankcase vent valve / oil separator + hoses ($60-200)
  2. Intake boot / throttle body boot leak ($30-150)
  3. DISA valve seal ($30-150)
  4. Aged MAF ($150-300)

Check first: Both banks lean = central cause: smoke-test CCV system and intake boots. Replace the CCV as a known weak point on M54/N52.

Source: BimmerFest 'SOLVED P0171 P0174 P1083 P1085' thread
