C0035 — Left Front Wheel Speed Sensor Circuit

CAUTIONIs it safe to drive? Normal brakes work fine, but ABS and traction/stability control are likely disabled — no anti-lock protection in a panic stop. Drive with extra following distance.

What C0035 means

The ABS module uses four wheel-speed sensors to detect which wheel is locking up. A circuit fault on the left front disables ABS (and usually traction and stability control) as a safety default. The basic brake system is unaffected.

Most likely causes (in order)

  1. Damaged or corroded wheel-speed sensor connector / wiring (most common — inspect the harness at the wheel)
  2. Failed wheel speed sensor
  3. Damaged reluctor ring (tone ring) on the hub or CV axle
  4. Wheel bearing failure (many sensors are built into the bearing)

Symptoms you might notice

  • ABS light on
  • Traction and stability control disabled
  • Speedometer error on some vehicles

What to check first

Inspect the harness at the left-front wheel — this wiring flexes every time the wheel steers and cracks first. Check the connector for corrosion. Measure sensor resistance (typically 1-2 kOhm passive, or verify a clean square-wave signal on active sensors).
