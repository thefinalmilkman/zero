P0402 — Exhaust Gas Recirculation Flow Excessive

SAFEIs it safe to drive? Emissions-related fault that won't leave you stranded — drive normally but get it diagnosed before your next inspection.

What P0402 means

The EGR system recirculates a metered amount of exhaust gas back into the intake to lower combustion temperatures and reduce NOx emissions. 'Excessive flow' means too much EGR is getting in — often a valve stuck open or a position sensor reading incorrectly.

Most likely causes (in order)

  1. EGR valve stuck open (carbon buildup holding it open — clean or replace)
  2. EGR position sensor faulty (tells ECM valve is closed when it's open)
  3. EGR passages clogged with carbon causing pressure differential issues
  4. DPFE sensor (Ford) failed or hoses swapped/cracked
  5. Vacuum line leak allowing EGR to open unintentionally

Symptoms you might notice

  • rough idle
  • stumble at low speed
  • hesitation on tip-in
  • occasional stall

What to check first

Disconnect the EGR vacuum line (or command the valve closed on a scan tool) and see if idle quality improves immediately — if it smooths out, the valve is stuck open or leaking through.
