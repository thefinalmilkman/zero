P1105 — Fuel Pressure Regulator / Solenoid Control

CAUTIONIs it safe to drive? Drive gently and get it looked at soon — it can worsen or fail inspection.

What P1105 means

This is a manufacturer-specific code — its meaning depends on the vehicle’s make (see below).

What P1105 means on specific makes

Mitsubishi CAUTION

Fault in the fuel pressure regulator control solenoid circuit (the duty-cycle solenoid that manages fuel-rail pressure on certain Mitsubishi systems). Can cause rich/lean and driveability issues. Note: some refs tie P1105 to a barometric/MAP-related solenoid — confirm by model.

  1. Failed fuel pressure regulator solenoid ($60-200)
  2. Connector/wiring fault
  3. Vacuum reference leak

Check first: Test the solenoid and its vacuum reference; watch fuel pressure and trims. Verify the exact function against the model's service info.

Source: troublecodes.net/mitsu, ricksfreeautorepairadvice.com/mitsubishi-obd2-codes
